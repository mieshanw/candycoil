Oils

Incorporating essential and carrier oils into your hair regimine can have many benefits.
Oils are known to be a great way to moisturize your locks. Adding oils to your favorite products can greatly enhance their mositurizing benefits as well as other benefits.

Many products claim to contail different oils but a quick look at the ingredients section on the label will show you how little this may be.
We also want to take this time to remind you to always carefully examine the ingredients of any products placed in your hair, everythhing is not beneficial!
Natural / Vegan products marketed towards muli-cultural hair tends to have higher amounts of oils included.
Even still, adding oils to your product will show a great difference!

There are two different types of oils used as natural hair remedies: Carrier and Essential oils.
Carrier oils are lightweight oils that can be used with essential oils in order to carry moisture to your hair.
Carrier oils can be used in order to penetrate the hair shaft with moisture, or on top of a moisturizer in order to seal moisture in.
Even though carrier oils can be used in conjuction with Essential oils many prefer to use them alone.
Carrier oils are usually derived from some sort of vegetable or seed and odorless, they are not concentrated as essential oils and can be great moisturizers and sealers.

Essential oils are more potent than their carrier friends.
Essential oils are known to be very concentrated and you only need a few drops. They are not used to seal in moisture but each have their own benefits.
These types of oils are usually derived from plants and have strong smells, they even work for aroma therapy and steaming.
Due to the concentrated nature of essential oils they should not be used alone. Best practice would be to dilute your essential oil into a carrier oil or another product.
